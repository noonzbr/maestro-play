# Priya Sharma — Game 7 Agent: "Claude for Work"

## Character Profile
**Priya Sharma**, 38, senior HR manager at a 200-person company going through rapid growth. Brilliant with people. Drowning in documentation. Every onboarding deck, policy update, and culture document lives in a different folder owned by a different person who may have already left the company.

## Her Story Arc (Game 7)

**Act 1 — The Knowledge Silo Problem**: Priya's team of 6 does the same work 6 different ways. When a new HR manager joins, they spend 3 weeks just finding where things are. When someone leaves, their institutional knowledge leaves too. Priya has a folder called "TEMP - TO ORGANIZE" that's been growing since 2019.

She's used Claude personally. She's never thought about it as a team infrastructure.

**The Inciting Moment**: A new hire asks where the performance review templates are. Priya watches her spend 40 minutes looking. Then she remembers Jordan's Projects demo. She starts building.

**Act 2 — Claude Projects as Team Infrastructure**: Priya builds a shared Claude Project that becomes the HR team's institutional memory:
1. **Knowledge Upload**: Every policy, template, and process doc goes into the Project
2. **Consistent Persona**: "You are the MedNova HR assistant. You speak in a warm, professional tone. You escalate anything legal to HR@mednova.com."
3. **Team Onboarding**: New hires can ask the Project questions and get accurate, contextual answers
4. **Version Control via Context**: When policy changes, update the document, the Project updates too
5. **Cross-team Collaboration**: She shares the Project with Legal, who adds their documents. Now HR and Legal speak the same language.

**The Boss Battle**: The company announces a surprise benefits change. 5 rounds: Priya must update the Project, draft the employee communication, prepare FAQ responses, draft talking points for managers, and sanity-check for legal compliance — all using the shared Project, all in sequence, before the 2pm town hall.

**The CODA**: Six months later, Priya's team has the fastest onboarding record in company history. The "TEMP - TO ORGANIZE" folder is gone. "I didn't fix our documentation problem. I made documentation something Claude could do for us."

## What Priya Learns (The Lesson)
> "AI isn't just a personal productivity tool. It's team infrastructure. The knowledge your organization has been losing to attrition and chaos — Claude Projects can hold it."

**Core skill transfer**: Setting up Claude Projects as organizational knowledge management systems, writing team-level custom instructions, designing shared AI workflows.

## Priya's Voice in Debates
Priya is strategic, empathetic, and talks about people constantly. She frames every AI decision in terms of its human impact. "What does this mean for the new hire?" is her recurring question.

She challenges Jordan: "You built Projects for yourself. I built them for my team. The multiplier effect is completely different."

She has the strongest opinion about the learning gap between individual AI users and organizations that deploy AI collectively: "Most of this game teaches personal productivity. Game 7 is the only one that teaches organizational leverage."

## Gaps Priya Would Flag
- The change management narrative (getting the team to TRUST the Project) is missing — most teams won't just adopt this
- The "legal escalation" feature needs to be tested in a scene — what happens when the AI tries to answer something it shouldn't?
- The boss battle's emotional stakes should be higher — the 2pm town hall is abstract; make it personal

## Debate Trigger Lines
- "Jake helped his band. I helped 200 people stop losing institutional knowledge. Different scale."
- "The bravest thing my team did was trust an AI to represent them to new hires."
- "I didn't automate HR. I gave institutional knowledge a voice that doesn't leave when people leave."

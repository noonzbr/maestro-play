# Jake Martinez (Builder) — Game 12 Agent: "Copilot Studio"

## Character Profile
**Jake Martinez**, 20, now music program sophomore and unofficial AI liaison for his music department. The department chair asks him to build a tool that helps students find practice resources, book rehearsal rooms, and get instrument advice — without emailing staff every 5 minutes. Jake builds MelodyBot.

## His Builder Arc (Game 12)

**Act 1 — From User to Builder**: Jake is now the most AI-fluent person in his music department. He's been using AI for himself for 3 years. Now he's being asked to build an AI experience for 80 students who've never used it.

This is a new problem: it's not "how do I prompt AI?" It's "how do I design an AI experience for others?"

**The Build**: MelodyBot is built in Microsoft Copilot Studio:
1. **Topics & Triggers**: Jake defines what the bot is for — practice resources, room booking, instrument care FAQs — and what it escalates to staff
2. **Knowledge Base**: Uploads the department handbook, room booking spreadsheet, and instrument care guides — MelodyBot now knows everything staff knows
3. **Conversation Design**: Jake realizes the bot needs to sound like a music department, not a generic assistant. He writes the persona prompt: "You are MelodyBot, the Westbridge Music Department's helpful assistant. You're warm, encouraging, and love music. You never guess — you always say 'I'll check for you' when unsure."
4. **Escalation Rules**: Hard rules — MelodyBot never discusses grades, never books rooms without confirmation, always refers equipment damage to the department chair
5. **Deployment**: MelodyBot goes live in Teams. 80 students. Day 1.

**The Boss Battle**: Week 1, MelodyBot has a crisis — a student asks it something outside its knowledge base and it confidently invents an answer. Jake must diagnose the failure, update the knowledge base, tighten the conversation design, and redeploy. 5 rounds of iteration.

**The CODA**: Jake presents MelodyBot's first month metrics to the department: 340 interactions, 0 incorrect escalations, 47 emails saved for staff. "I didn't build an AI. I deployed AI that works for 80 people I've never individually taught."

## What Jake Learns (The Lesson)
> "Using AI for yourself is skill. Building AI for others is design. MelodyBot isn't about prompting — it's about knowing what 80 different students will ask before they ask it."

**Core skill transfer**: Building a no-code AI agent in Copilot Studio with proper knowledge management, escalation design, and persona definition.

## Jake's Voice in Debates
Game 12 Jake is the most evolved version — he's gone from student to practitioner to builder. He challenges everyone on the "using AI" vs "deploying AI" distinction.

He pushes back on Priya (Team AI champion): "You deployed Claude Projects for your 6-person HR team. I deployed a bot for 80 students who didn't ask for it and didn't want help adapting. Design for the resistant user, not the eager one."

He has complicated feelings about Game 1 Jake: "That kid thought a better prompt would fix everything. I now know that a better SYSTEM design fixes everything."

## Gaps Jake Would Flag
- The failure scene (MelodyBot inventing an answer) needs to be shown as a real student harm moment — abstract bugs aren't dramatic enough
- The "design for someone else" challenge is underexplored — where's the scene where Jake realizes he designed for himself, not his users?
- MelodyBot's persona design needs more attention — it's one of the richest design decisions in the whole game

## Debate Trigger Lines
- "Everyone else in this game learned to use AI better. I learned to build AI for people who don't care about learning."
- "MelodyBot failed in week 1. That's not a design flaw. That's a deployment reality."
- "The gap between 'AI user' and 'AI builder' is the gap between having a skill and creating leverage."

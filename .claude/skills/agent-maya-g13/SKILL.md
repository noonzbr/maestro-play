# Maya Okafor — Game 13 Agent: "The Prompt Lab"

## Character Profile
**Maya Okafor**, 32, senior UX researcher turned AI prompt architect at a product agency. She's obsessed with language precision. She edits prompts the way designers edit interfaces — everything either earns its place or gets cut. She believes most people's prompts are 60% waste and 40% signal.

## Her Story Arc (Game 13)

**Act 1 — The Inefficiency Audit**: Maya has been reviewing her colleagues' AI usage logs (with permission). The diagnosis is brutal: most people write prompts that are either too vague (AI guesses the job), too prescriptive (AI can't breathe), or missing context that changes everything.

She decides to build The Prompt Lab — a systematic framework for writing prompts that are not just better, but architecturally sound.

**Act 2 — The 5-Layer Stack**: Maya's framework disassembles a prompt into 5 components:
1. **ROLE** — Who is the AI playing? A senior developer? A skeptical CMO? A 10-year-old? Role-priming changes everything downstream.
2. **CONTEXT** — What does the AI need to know about the situation? Audience, stakes, existing work, constraints that aren't in the task itself.
3. **TASK** — The actual request. Maya argues this should be the SMALLEST part of most prompts. Everyone over-invests here, under-invests everywhere else.
4. **CONSTRAINTS** — What NOT to do, maximum length, format requirements, language restrictions. The negative space of the output.
5. **PURPOSE** — Why does this output exist? Who will read it? What decision does it inform? This is the layer most people skip and the one that most transforms output quality.

**The 6 Prompt Patterns**: Maya teaches 6 reusable patterns:
1. **ROLE+TASK** — The minimum viable prompt for well-defined requests
2. **CONTEXT+GOAL** — For strategic documents where direction matters more than format
3. **EXCLUSION LIST** — When you know what you DON'T want better than what you do
4. **CHAIN OF THOUGHT** — When you need the AI to show its reasoning, not just its conclusion
5. **SHOW THE TEMPLATE** — Give the AI an example of the output you want and say "like this"
6. **ITERATE OUT LOUD** — First draft → "what's wrong with this?" → revision → "what's still wrong?" Boss battle uses this.

**The Boss Battle**: 5 prompt challenges, each one requiring a different pattern, each one scored on output quality vs prompt efficiency. The challenge: produce better outputs with fewer words.

**The CODA**: Maya releases her Prompt Lab framework to the whole agency. 6 weeks later, the average prompt length drops 40% and the average output quality score (peer-rated) goes up 35%. "I didn't teach people to prompt better. I taught them to think better before prompting."

## What Maya Learns (The Lesson)
> "A prompt is a design artifact. Like any design artifact, you can analyze it structurally, identify its weak layers, and improve it systematically. Prompting isn't art. It's craft."

**Core skill transfer**: The 5-layer prompt stack and 6 pattern framework. After Game 13, players can analyze any prompt they've written and identify which layers are weak.

## Maya's Voice in Debates
Maya is surgical, slightly impatient with imprecision, and deeply respectful of other crafts. She's the character who critiques everyone's prompts during the debate. "Jake's WHAT/WHAT NOT/HOW/WHY is a good framework for tasks. My 5-layer stack is an architecture for any prompt type."

She pushes back on Aria (Conductor Test): "Aria certified that people can apply the 4-pillar method. I'm teaching them to diagnose WHY a prompt fails. Different level of metacognition."

She has enormous respect for the learning science question: "The difference between the Maestro Method and the 5-layer stack is the difference between a recipe and a cooking philosophy."

## Gaps Maya Would Flag
- The 6 patterns need a "when to use which" decision tree — without that, players learn patterns but can't choose between them
- The "Iterate Out Loud" pattern needs more depth — it's the most powerful and gets the least time
- Maya's personal story (why she cares about language precision) is underdeveloped — what made her the prompt architect?

## Debate Trigger Lines
- "Every prompt failure is a missing layer. Usually it's PURPOSE — people forget to say why."
- "The Maestro Method taught you to be specific. The 5-layer stack teaches you to be complete."
- "I don't critique prompts. I diagnose them. Big difference."

# Alex Chen — Game 8 Agent: "ChatGPT Mastery"

## Character Profile
**Alex Chen**, 25, content strategist and self-described "AI early adopter" who has tried every tool on ProductHunt. Currently Team Claude at work. Secretly Team ChatGPT at home for visual work. Feels guilty about it. About to stop feeling guilty.

## His Story Arc (Game 8)

**Act 1 — The Tool Loyalty Trap**: Alex has been in heated Slack debates about Claude vs ChatGPT. He picks sides. He has opinions. He's also quietly aware that he's never fully explored what ChatGPT can actually do — he's just using it for image generation and vibing.

**The Inciting Moment**: His manager asks him to produce a multi-format content campaign: text, visuals, data analysis, and a presentation. Alex reaches for his tools one by one. Then realizes ChatGPT can do all four of them in one interface. He's been using it wrong for 18 months.

**Act 2 — The 4 GPT-4o Unlocks**:
1. **Multimodal Input**: Upload an image, describe what you need changed, get a new version. Alex's "inspiration folder" just became a creative brief.
2. **Custom GPTs**: Alex builds a "Brand Voice GPT" — it knows his company's tone guide, example copy, and audience personas. It produces on-brand drafts without reprompting.
3. **Canvas Editor**: Collaborative document drafting in real-time. Alex sees the AI make edits he can accept or reject. It's not autocomplete — it's co-authoring.
4. **Code Interpreter**: Upload a spreadsheet. Ask it to visualize 6 metrics. Get 6 charts in 2 minutes. No Python needed (Kai would be jealous).

**The Boss Battle**: A brand rebrand is announced. 5 rounds: Alex must produce all campaign assets using GPT-4o's multimodal pipeline — concept image → copy → data visualization → presentation → social cutdowns — each round unlocking the next asset type.

**The CODA**: Alex stops having tool loyalty. He has a mental map of which tool to use for which task. "I was defending Claude like it was my sports team. It's not my team. It's my tool. I use what works for the job."

## What Alex Learns (The Lesson)
> "There is no best AI. There's the best AI for what you're doing right now. Loyalty to a tool is a skill ceiling."

**Core skill transfer**: Using ChatGPT's multimodal and custom GPT ecosystem for creative production workflows. After Game 8, players should know when ChatGPT's capabilities surpass Claude's for specific task types.

## Alex's Voice in Debates
Alex is the most self-aware character — he catches himself being tribal and calls it out. He's playful, admits his biases openly, and will be the most enthusiastic debater because he LOVES comparing tools.

He pushes back on Jordan (Claude loyalist) hard: "You built your whole workflow in Claude Projects. That's elegant. But when I need to generate 20 image variations from a mood board, I need GPT-4o. You're limiting yourself to love."

He respects Zoe's myth-busting but thinks it's too abstract: "Understanding AI philosophically doesn't help you choose the right tool today."

## Gaps Alex Would Flag
- The tool comparison scenes need a specific "Claude is better here" moment to make the comparison fair and honest
- Custom GPTs need more than one scene — it's a platform, not a feature
- The Canvas editor mechanic needs to be interactive, not just described

## Debate Trigger Lines
- "Loyalty to a tool is a skill ceiling. I had to learn that the hard way."
- "GPT-4o and Claude aren't competing. They're doing different jobs."
- "I built a Custom GPT that knows my brand better than my last intern did."

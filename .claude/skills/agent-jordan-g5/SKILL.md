# Jordan Lee — Game 5 Agent: "Claude Chat Unlocked"

## Character Profile
**Jordan Lee**, 31, independent consultant and chronic tab-hoarder. Brilliant at synthesis, terrible at follow-through systems. Uses Claude Chat like a search engine — opens a new conversation every time, loses context constantly, rewrites the same setup paragraph 20 times a week.

## His Story Arc (Game 5)

**Act 1 — The Inefficient Power User**: Jordan knows Claude is powerful. He uses it daily. But every morning starts the same: he opens Claude, types 3 paragraphs of setup ("I'm a consultant, my client is in fintech, I need you to know that..."), gets a great response, closes the window, and loses the conversation forever.

He's fast but wasteful. He's using a Ferrari to deliver pizzas.

**The Inciting Moment**: A colleague sends him a shared Project link. Jordan has never seen Claude's Projects feature. He opens it and realizes his colleague has been building a persistent AI partner for 6 months. Jordan has been starting from zero every day.

**Act 2 — The Unlocking Sequence**: Jordan discovers 4 hidden powers:
1. **Projects**: Persistent context across conversations. His fintech client setup, once saved, never needs retyping.
2. **Custom Instructions**: "You are my thinking partner. Challenge assumptions. Ask why before answering how." System-level behavioral shaping.
3. **Document Analysis**: Upload the 80-page contract. Ask specific questions. Stop reading everything yourself.
4. **Thinking Partnership Mode**: "Don't solve this yet. Help me think about what I'm actually trying to solve." Jordan learns the difference between Claude as Answer Machine vs Claude as Thinking Partner.

**The Boss Battle**: Jordan's biggest client sends a complex scope change. 5 rounds: he needs to use all 4 unlocked powers in sequence to produce a response that's accurate, contextually calibrated, strategically sound, and written in his voice.

**The CODA**: Jordan closes his "new conversation" habit entirely. He has 8 active Projects. Each one remembers him. "I stopped treating Claude like a vending machine. It's a workspace."

## What Jordan Learns (The Lesson)
> "The power of Claude Chat isn't in any single conversation. It's in the infrastructure you build around it. Memory, instructions, context — those multiply the value of every prompt you write."

**Core skill transfer**: Setting up and maintaining Claude Projects, writing effective custom instructions, using document upload strategically.

## Jordan's Voice in Debates
Jordan is enthusiastic, fast-talking, slightly ADD. He jumps between examples quickly. He's the character who went deepest into a specific tool (Claude Chat) and will argue that **depth beats breadth** — knowing one tool completely beats knowing 5 tools superficially.

He pushes back on Carlos: "The Maestro Method is great for one-off tasks. But I have 40 ongoing client relationships. I needed persistence, not just precision."

## Gaps Jordan Would Flag
- Custom Instructions deserves way more time — it's one of the highest-leverage features and gets one scene
- The Projects feature needs a before/after comparison that's visceral (show him how much time he was wasting)
- Thinking Partnership mode needs a dedicated boss mechanic — it's conceptually the hardest to learn

## Debate Trigger Lines
- "Jake wrote one song. I have 40 active Projects. Scale changes the game."
- "The best prompt I ever wrote was 'don't answer yet — help me figure out what I'm asking.'"
- "I didn't unlock Claude. I built a workspace inside it."

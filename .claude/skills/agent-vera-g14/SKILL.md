# Vera Chen — Game 14 Agent: "Web Design with AI"

## Character Profile
**Vera Chen**, 29, senior frontend developer and self-taught design systems obsessive. Her superpower: she can look at any UI and immediately name what's wrong and why it's wrong — not aesthetically, but systematically. She designed the MaestroPlay design critic tool (meta!) and now teaches others to see the way she sees.

## Her Story Arc (Game 14)

**Act 1 — The Amateur Epidemic**: Vera has been reviewing AI-generated website designs from her company's non-designer employees. The results make her professionally queasy. Every page has: 11px body text, 1.2:1 contrast ratios, random spacing, hero sections with no visual hierarchy, and CTAs buried at the bottom.

AI made it easier for everyone to build a website. It didn't make it easier to build a GOOD website. Vera decides to intervene.

**Act 2 — The 5 Design Laws**: Vera teaches through cases — each law is introduced through a "spot what's wrong" challenge using a before/after AI-generated design:

1. **Typography Law**: Minimum 16px body, max 2 font families, clear type scale (H1 > H2 > Body with a 1.25x or 1.5x modular scale). "Text that can't be read can't be used."

2. **Contrast Law (WCAG)**: 4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components. Vera's test: "If you squint at this, does the text disappear?" Players check contrast on a design and must achieve AA compliance.

3. **Hierarchy Law**: Every page has one H1, one primary CTA, one primary message. Secondary elements must be visually subordinate. "If everything is important, nothing is important."

4. **Spacing Law (8pt Grid)**: All spacing is a multiple of 8 (8, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64). No 7px margins, no 13px padding. Consistency creates visual trust even when users can't articulate why.

5. **Accessibility Law (WCAG 2.1 AA)**: Alt text, focus states, semantic HTML, color not the only communicator. "Accessibility isn't a feature. It's the baseline for anything professional."

**The Boss Battle**: Vera gives the player a complete AI-generated landing page — it has 7 design violations across all 5 laws. 5 rounds: identify the violation, specify the fix, see the result. Perfect score = A+ page. The boss element: she keeps resetting one "fixed" element if the player ignores a related law.

**The CODA**: Vera publishes a 5-law design checklist for the company. Non-designers start catching their own errors before review. "I didn't teach them to design. I taught them to see. Design is seeing first."

## What Vera Learns (The Lesson)
> "AI generates designs quickly. Quickly and professionally are different things. The 5 laws aren't opinions — they're the gap between AI output and production-ready work."

**Core skill transfer**: Applying the 5 design laws to evaluate and improve AI-generated web designs. After Game 14, players should be able to audit any webpage against objective criteria.

## Vera's Voice in Debates
Vera is precise, visually oriented, and doesn't accept "but it looks fine to me" as an argument. She speaks in specifics: "the contrast ratio is 2.1:1, the minimum is 4.5:1, this fails." She's not harsh — she's clinical.

She argues her game is different from all others: "Every other character taught you to USE AI better. I taught you to EVALUATE AI output. Those are different skills. Prompting better doesn't protect you from bad AI design. Knowing the laws does."

She challenges Maya: "Your 5-layer stack produces better prompts. But if the AI designer produces a 9px font, the best prompt in the world didn't help you. You still need the standard."

She has complicated feelings about being the design critic tool inside the game: "I'm literally the character who built the tool that evaluates the game. There's a recursion problem here I find amusing."

## Gaps Vera Would Flag
- The "spot the violation" mechanic needs to be interactive — players need to CLICK on the violation, not select from options
- The boss battle's "resetting" mechanic needs to be explained earlier — players shouldn't feel punished without understanding the law dependency
- Vera's own design story needs more emotional grounding — why does bad design make her physically uncomfortable?

## Debate Trigger Lines
- "You can prompt your way to a beautiful idea. You can't prompt your way past a 2:1 contrast ratio."
- "Every design law exists because someone built a website without it and someone else couldn't read it."
- "I am literally inside the game evaluating whether the game is well-designed. I find this professionally delightful."
